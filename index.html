College of Arts & Media Showcase Project

University of Colorado | Denver | Katie Ryan

This is a responsive design project and was created using Figma. The prototypes show the potential for creating a portfolio website for the students and alumni of the CAM Department at CU Denver. Goals of the project are to help students and alumni gain exposure to industry professionals by showcasing their portfolios, to foster potential client relations by offering contact information for each artist, to learn about artists and media creators local to Denver, to show prospective students the masterpieces of our students and peers, and to foster love of creativity and community.

Mobile Prototype

This is the Mobile prototype of the showcase project. 63% of website traffic is from mobile devices, so it is important to have a responsive design that works well on all devices. This prototype was created using Figma and is fully interactive. The goal of this project is to create a portfolio website for the students and alumni of the CAM Department at CU Denver.

Features

The features section of a website is an important part of the design. It is where you can showcase the key features and benefits of using your website.

Accessibility

Follows WCAG guidelines and is accessible

Cross-Platform

Able to open on all browsers

Consistent Branding

Follows brand guidelines across all devices

Contact Information

Includes contact for each artist including but not limited to email, social media, and personal websites. This is not mandatory, but offered to students who choose to include that information.

Filter Search Function

Allows for more advanced search option on the desktop version.

Links to Socials & Websites

In the contact information section, there are clickable links to the artist's social media and personal websites.

Call to Action

Emphasizes contact for the artists and encourages users to reach out, thus creating client relations.

About Me Section

The artist page includes an about me section that allows the artist to share their story, background, and any other relevant information.

Microinteractions

Microinteractions are small, subtle animations that occur when a user interacts with a website. They can be used to provide feedback, guide users, and enhance the overall user experience.
